My experience : a little more about me

My first co-op as a Software Test Engineer at Netgear was not a typical coding job; it was really hands-on, and as a result, I took away a lot more from the experience. There were so many networking concepts I didn’t know going into the job, and I had to figure them out by experimenting and learning as I went. I was responsible for bringing up an entire test chamber on my own, including network configurations and topologies, and had to consider the end goal: what we were trying to achieve and how to make the setup convenient for the team. I took it upon myself to streamline processes, automate routine firmware tests, and reduce a full day of work to about two hours of offline testing. I also trained other co-ops to continue the workflow, which was rewarding and taught me to be self-reliant, think critically, and develop practical, efficient solutions. In the end, it was rewarding to see how much impact small improvements could have on the team’s productivity and efficiency.
