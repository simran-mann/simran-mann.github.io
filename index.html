Experience

SFU Robot Soccer

At SFU Robot Soccer, I work on the development, testing, and debugging of firmware for the robot motherboards and basestation, ensuring the robots receive commands from the AI agent developed by the software team and respond in real time. I designed and implemented the basestation firmware to receive and parse packets from the software team and relay them over RF to the robots, which then schedule tasks and execute commands accordingly. Working in this team spanning firmware, software, and electronics has taught me a lot about bare-metal C programming, system integration, and effective teamwork.

NETGEAR

My first co-op as a Software Test Engineer at Netgear was not a typical coding job; it was really hands-on, and as a result, I took away a lot more from the experience. I was responsible for bringing up an entire test chamber on my own, including network configurations and topologies, and had to consider the end goal: what we were trying to achieve and how to make the setup convenient for the team.

I took it upon myself to streamline processes, automate routine firmware tests, and reduce a full day of work to about two hours of offline testing. I also trained other co-ops to continue the workflow, which was rewarding and taught me to be self-reliant, think critically, and develop practical, efficient solutions. In the end, it was rewarding to see how much impact small improvements could have on the team's productivity and efficiency.
